## 3. Role Overview and Mandate

The Digital Transformation Officer leads the modernisation of the Grenada Meteorological Service's digital infrastructure, operational systems, data management, and public communication platforms.

The role exists to:
- Build and operate the digital systems that enable GMS to produce, store, and disseminate timely, accurate, and actionable weather and climate information.
- Implement an Impact-Based Forecasting and Warning System (IBFWS) and CAP-compliant alerting infrastructure.
- Establish a Meteorological and Climate Data Management System (MDMS).
- Develop public-facing platforms for forecast and warning dissemination.
- Digitise and automate operational workflows to reduce manual effort and increase service reliability.
- Ensure alignment with WMO, ICAO, and national disaster risk reduction standards.

The programme scope is the **Meteorology Department and its digital systems**. Extension to other GAA departments requires written approval from the CEO (see Section 17).

## 6. Key Responsibilities

### 1. Digital Strategy and Programme Management
- Develop and maintain the GMS multi-year Digital Transformation Strategy and annual implementation plans.
- Manage the digital programme roadmap, backlog, and release schedule.
- Identify and implement opportunities for automation, data-driven operations, and digital efficiency.

### 2. Meteorological Data Management (MDMS / CDMS)
- Implement and maintain the SURFACE Climate and Meteorological Data Management System (an open-source CDMS with regional Caribbean precedent) as the department's centralised data store.
- Ensure automated ingestion, quality control, archiving, and metadata management for all meteorological and hydrological data.
- Integrate AWS, marine, and hydrology data streams into operational platforms.

### 3. Impact-Based Forecasting and Warning System (IBFWS)
- Lead the digital enabling of IBFWS, incorporating hazard, exposure, and vulnerability datasets.
- Develop impact matrices, digital risk maps, and sector-specific forecast products.
- Implement CAP-compliant automated alerting and multi-channel dissemination (SMS, website, WhatsApp, email, social media).
- Support forecasters in producing impact-based advisories and warnings.

### 4. Hazardous Weather and Climate Events Catalogue
- Integrate CMO/CIMH Severe Weather Catalogue data with a departmental Hazard, Impact, and Climate Events Database.
- Implement standardised coding for events, impacts, locations, and affected sectors.
- Automate production of annual climate and extreme-event summary reports.

### 5. Digital Infrastructure and Cybersecurity
- Maintain and develop ICT infrastructure including cloud platforms, databases, and storage.
- Develop and enforce cybersecurity protocols, backup procedures, and disaster recovery plans in collaboration with ISDS.
- Monitor system performance, uptime, and integrity.

### 6. Public-Facing Digital Platforms
- Develop and maintain the department's public weather website and forecast dissemination platform.
- Build dashboards for real-time data visualisation and public access.
- Improve user experience, accessibility, and reach of forecasting products.

### 7. Workflow Automation and Operational Tools
- Digitise operational processes for forecast production, bulletin generation, and interagency communication.
- Build and maintain automated scripts, templates, and data-processing systems.
- Standardise digital formats for climate services and sector-specific products.
- Pilot AI/ML tools for nowcasting, rainfall prediction, and anomaly detection (Year 2+).
- Trial IoT sensor network integrations (rain gauges, coastal sensors) where resources permit.

### 8. Interagency and Regional Coordination
- Establish digital data-sharing mechanisms with Aviation, Disaster Office, Water Authority, Marine, Energy, and other sectors.
- Ensure alignment with WMO WIS2, CIMH, CARIBE-EWS, and regional climate services frameworks.
- Support national Multi-Hazard Early Warning System (MHEWS) initiatives.

### 9. Capacity Building
- Deliver training for staff in digital tools, data literacy, GIS, cybersecurity, and IBFWS workflows.
- Provide ongoing technical support and digital skills mentoring.
- Document operational procedures and system manuals for handover continuity.

## 7. Decision Authority

### Autonomous Authority (DTO)

The DTO may independently decide, within approved scope and budget:
- Software architecture and frameworks used for Meteorology systems.
- Technical design for internal dashboards, tools, and integrations.
- Ordering and prioritisation of the product backlog within agreed priorities.
- Scheduling and coordinating releases that do not affect core GAA infrastructure or policies.

### Collaborative Authority (DTO and ISDS)

The following require collaborative decision-making with ISDS:
- Cloud deployment configuration for production or externally exposed systems.
- DNS records, SSL certificates, and related network routing.
- Identity, access management, and permission frameworks.
- Security configurations and firewall-related changes.
- DevOps pipeline configurations that interact with core GAA infrastructure.

The expectation is collaborative resolution rather than unilateral veto, except where security policies mandate otherwise. Where ISDS approval is required, a target response time of **five (5) working days** is expected; delays beyond this should be escalated to the Manager, ISDS.

### Approval Required (Management / CEO)

- Any cloud expenditure increase above the agreed development-phase cap.
- Procurement of paid software, SaaS platforms, or third-party services.
- Contracts with external vendors, consultants, or service providers.
- Expansion of scope beyond the Meteorology Department.
