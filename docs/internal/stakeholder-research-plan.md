# Stakeholder and User Research Plan

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Status** | Draft |
| **Owner** | Digital Transformation Officer |
| **Purpose** | Guide stakeholder interviews and research to inform GMS digital product design |
| **Target completion** | Q3 2026 |

> GMS digital products should be designed around real decisions, not meteorological variables. This plan identifies the stakeholder groups, the decisions they make, and the questions that help us understand how digital GMS products should be shaped.

---

## Principles

**Design around decisions, not data.** Every product should be traceable to a decision that a real person or institution needs to make. The question is not "what data does the user want?" but "what action or decision does this information enable?"

**Understand thresholds, not just preferences.** For each stakeholder group, identify the specific meteorological or warning thresholds that change their behaviour. These thresholds become the basis of impact-based forecasting parameters.

**Capture existing information pathways.** Understand where stakeholders currently get weather information and why they trust or distrust it. This reveals what GMS digital products need to replace or improve.

**Include dissatisfaction as a signal.** What do stakeholders currently get wrong, misunderstand, or act on incorrectly? These gaps are the highest-value product design opportunities.

## Stakeholder Groups and Core Questions

### 1. General Public

**Primary decisions:**
- What should I do today given expected weather?
- Is it safe to travel / be outdoors / hold an event?
- Should I prepare for a hazard?

**Interview questions:**
1. How do you currently get weather information?
2. Which sources do you trust most, and why?
3. Can you describe a time when you made a wrong decision because of weather — what information did you have, and what did you expect?
4. What does a weather warning mean to you — what do you do when you see one?
5. What is the difference between an advisory, a watch, and a warning in your understanding?
6. How do you receive weather updates during a tropical storm or hurricane?
7. What would make you trust a weather source more?
8. What do you wish you knew about weather that you currently don't?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Channel preferences (Facebook, radio, WhatsApp, website)
- Warning response behaviour
- Understanding of advisory/watch/warning levels
- Trust signals

---

### 2. NaDMA / NDEMA (National Disaster Management Agency)

**Primary decisions:**
- When should we activate the Emergency Operations Centre?
- When should we issue public emergency alerts or evacuation orders?
- What resources should we pre-position and where?
- When should we escalate to Cabinet?

**Interview questions:**
1. Walk me through how you use weather information before, during, and after a hazardous event.
2. What is the specific weather threshold or warning level that triggers your activation procedures?
3. In what format and channel do you need to receive official GMS warnings to act on them?
4. What information is missing from current GMS products that would help your decisions?
5. How do you communicate GMS warnings downstream to communities and partner agencies?
6. What has gone wrong in the past when weather information was unclear, late, or inconsistent?
7. Would a direct CAP feed from GMS into your systems be useful? What would it need to contain?
8. What are your lead time requirements — how early do you need a warning to act?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Activation thresholds by hazard type
- Required lead times
- CAP adoption potential
- Desired format (graphic, text, structured data, briefing)

---

### 3. Grenada Airports Authority / Airport Management

**Primary decisions:**
- Is the airport safe for operations?
- Should we delay, divert, or cancel flights?
- What should we communicate to airlines and ATC?

**Interview questions:**
1. What aviation meteorological products from GMS do you use daily, and which are most critical?
2. What format do you prefer for receiving TAFs, METARs, and aerodrome warnings?
3. How much advance notice do you need for an aerodrome warning to be operationally useful?
4. What information from GMS is currently missing or arriving too late?
5. How do you currently receive GMS aviation products — is the process reliable?
6. Can you describe a weather-related operational disruption and what information helped or hindered your decision?
7. What would an aviation dashboard at GMS need to show to be useful to airport operations?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Preferred product format (coded vs. plain language)
- Timing requirements for each product
- Dashboard feature priorities
- Dissemination gaps

---

### 4. Air Traffic Control (ATC)

**Primary decisions:**
- What routing, separation, or holding instructions should I give pilots?
- Are conditions within limits for the traffic I am managing?
- What hazardous weather information should I pass to approaching or departing aircraft?

**Interview questions:**
1. Which GMS products do you use in the ATC environment?
2. What are the specific weather triggers that cause you to take action (hold, divert, reroute)?
3. How do you currently receive METARs, SPECIs, and TAFs during your shift?
4. What is the acceptable latency for a SPECI to reach you after it is issued?
5. What wind shear or visibility information do you most need, and is it currently available in a useful format?
6. How would a consolidated aviation weather display improve your operations?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Latency requirements by product
- Wind shear and visibility thresholds
- Interface preferences

---

### 5. Airlines and Pilots

**Primary decisions:**
- Is it safe and efficient to operate this flight?
- What fuel reserves, alternate, or routing do I need?
- Should I delay departure or request a specific approach?

**Interview questions:**
1. What is your current pre-flight weather briefing process for flights to or from Grenada?
2. What GMS products do you use, and are they sufficient?
3. How current and accessible are GMS TAFs when you need them?
4. What weather information do you rely on that GMS currently does not provide?
5. What is the most important weather risk for Grenada operations in your experience?
6. Would you use a digital pre-flight weather portal for Grenada if one existed?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Briefing workflow and pain points
- TAF availability and timing gaps
- Desired digital access model

---

### 6. Fishers and Marine Users

**Primary decisions:**
- Is it safe to go to sea today?
- What sea areas, routes, or fishing grounds should I avoid?
- Should I return to port?

**Interview questions:**
1. How do you currently get marine weather information before going to sea?
2. What specific information — wind speed, wave height, sea state — do you use to decide whether to go out?
3. At what wind speed or wave height do you stay in port?
4. Do you currently receive any official marine forecast from GMS? If yes, how?
5. What language and format is most useful to you — coded forecast, plain language, graphic, voice?
6. Can you describe a time when you went to sea and the conditions were worse than expected — what information did you have?
7. How do you receive warnings when you are already at sea?
8. What weather information for Carriacou and Petite Martinique is currently missing?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Specific sea-state and wind thresholds per user segment
- Current information sources and trust levels
- Preferred delivery channel (WhatsApp, radio, SMS, website)
- Offshore reach of current GMS products

---

### 7. Schools and Education Sector

**Primary decisions:**
- Should school be closed or cancelled due to weather?
- Should outdoor activities or sports be postponed?
- How do we communicate weather risk to students and parents?

**Interview questions:**
1. How does your institution currently receive weather and warning information from GMS?
2. What information do you need to decide to close school due to weather?
3. What is the threshold — wind speed, warning level, advisory type — that triggers a closure decision?
4. Do you currently receive direct communication from NDEMA or GMS during tropical events?
5. What would make it easier to communicate weather risk to parents and students?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Closure decision thresholds
- Communication pathways
- Preferred format (notice, graphic, structured alert)

---

### 8. Farming and Agriculture Sector

**Primary decisions:**
- When should I plant, irrigate, or harvest?
- Should I spray pesticide or fertilizer today?
- Should I protect or harvest early because of an incoming hazard?
- How should I manage livestock in expected heat or storm conditions?

**Interview questions:**
1. What weather information do you currently use for your farming decisions?
2. Where do you currently get that information, and how reliable is it?
3. What specific weather conditions affect your most critical decisions — planting, spraying, harvesting?
4. At what rainfall amount or dry-spell length does your crop face risk?
5. Would you use a GMS mobile or WhatsApp service with farming-specific weather outlooks?
6. How far in advance do you need a rainfall or heat outlook to be useful?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Crop-specific meteorological thresholds
- Decision timing (days ahead needed)
- Channel preferences

---

### 9. Media (Broadcasters, Journalists)

**Primary decisions:**
- What weather information should I broadcast / publish today?
- How do I accurately communicate a warning without causing unnecessary panic?
- What graphics or summaries can I use?

**Interview questions:**
1. How do you currently receive information from GMS for broadcast or publication?
2. What format is most useful — plain text, embeddable graphic, structured data feed?
3. Do you ever receive unclear, contradictory, or late information from GMS that creates problems for your reporting?
4. What would a media-facing GMS product portal need to include to be useful?
5. How do you verify that a GMS warning is official before broadcasting it?
6. How do you handle the advisory / watch / warning distinction with your audience?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Content format requirements
- Authentication / verification needs
- Embeddable content requirements
- Warning language confusion patterns

---

### 10. Tourism and Hospitality Sector

**Primary decisions:**
- Should we cancel or reschedule outdoor tours, events, or activities?
- What should we communicate to guests about weather risk?
- Are cruise port conditions safe for port call operations?

**Interview questions:**
1. What weather risks most affect your operations — rain, wind, sea state, lightning?
2. How do you currently monitor weather before and during tours or events?
3. At what weather level do you cancel or reschedule activities, and what information triggers that decision?
4. Do you use GMS products currently? If not, what do you use instead?
5. How much notice do you need to safely communicate weather risk to guests?

**Design signals to capture:**
- Activity-specific thresholds
- Lead time requirements
- Preference for visual/graphic products

## Research Method

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Format** | Semi-structured interviews, 30–45 minutes per group |
| **Format option** | Group workshops where stakeholders share common decision needs (e.g. school sector) |
| **Recording** | Summary notes; no audio recording required |
| **Output** | Per-stakeholder decision threshold map and product design input |
| **Owner** | DTO coordinates; relevant GMS leads participate where technical content requires it |
| **Timeline** | Q3 2026 — complete at least NaDMA, airport, ATC, marine, and media groups before Warning and IBF Framework v2 |

---

## Output: Decision Threshold Register

For each stakeholder group, produce a one-page summary:

```yaml
stakeholderGroup: "Fishers and Marine Users"
primaryDecision: "Is it safe to go to sea today?"
keyThresholds:
  - windSpeed: "> 20 knots — reduce activity"
  - windSpeed: "> 30 knots — stay in port"
  - waveHeight: "> 2.0 m — avoid open water"
preferredChannel:
  - WhatsApp
  - Radio
  - SMS
currentSources:
  - "Regional radio"
  - "Trinidad weather app"
  - "Word of mouth"
gmsTrustLevel: "Low — products not reaching this group"
productDesignImplications:
  - "Plain-language advisory with specific wind and sea state numbers"
  - "WhatsApp-formatted short bulletin"
  - "Separate Carriacou/PM zone forecast"
```

This register feeds directly into the product content standards and dissemination channel decisions.
