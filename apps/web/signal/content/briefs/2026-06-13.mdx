## The Big Thing

A thick plume of **Saharan dust** is settling over Grenada this week, dimming
the skies and pushing air quality down. The Met Office expects hazy conditions
through Thursday. People with asthma and other respiratory conditions should
keep medication close and limit long stretches outdoors at midday.

## Weather Ready

Hot and hazy, light winds, a low chance of afternoon showers in the interior.
Seas are slight to moderate — a comfortable day on the water, but visibility
will be reduced by the dust. Farmers can expect strong sun; water young plants
early.

## Parish Pulse

Roadworks continue on the **Grand Anse main road** near the roundabout; expect
delays during morning peak. In **St. Patrick**, a community clean-up is set for
Saturday morning at Sauteurs bay.

## Opportunity

Applications are open for **Cuba medical scholarships** for the next academic
year. The deadline is at the end of the month — see Opportunity Watch for the
eligibility list and how to apply.

## Check D Ting

A message claiming **NAWASA announced an island-wide three-day water shutdown**
is circulating on WhatsApp. We checked: it's **false**. NAWASA has scheduled
maintenance affecting only parts of St. George on a single morning.

## What to Watch

Fuel prices are widely expected to edge up at the next review. Watch for the
official notice — and ignore the screenshots until then.
