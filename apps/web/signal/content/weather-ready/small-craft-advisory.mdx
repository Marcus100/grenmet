## What happened

The Met Office has issued a **small craft advisory** for Grenada, Carriacou, and
Petite Martinique. Seas are building to 2.5–3 metres with fresh winds, mainly on
the Atlantic side.

## Why it matters

A small craft advisory is not a storm warning. It's a heads-up that conditions
are **dangerous for small boats** — open fishing pirogues, small pleasure craft,
and dinghies — even when the sky looks fine.

## Who is affected

Fishers, ferry passengers between Grenada and the Sister Isles, dive and tour
operators, and anyone planning to take a small vessel out.

## What to do

- Delay non-essential trips in small craft until the advisory is lifted.
- If you must go out, wear a life jacket and tell someone your route and return
  time.
- Check the ferry operator's notices before travelling to Carriacou.
- Secure boats and gear at moorings.

The advisory is expected to remain in force until winds ease midweek.
