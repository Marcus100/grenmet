# Pre-season actions: General

By the last week of May each year, GAA should complete organizational readiness actions and verify preparedness through planning and exercises. {{ className: 'lead' }}

## Annual planning and readiness

- Update the GAA Tropical Cyclone Plan annually by the last week of May.
- Conduct a tabletop exercise across departments to assess readiness for the upcoming season.
- Conduct MBIA and Lauriston facility inspections and execute remedial work where needed.
- Establish a non-perishable food basket list and supplier arrangement for activation when the first watch advisory is issued.

## Damage and Needs Assessment (DANA) team

The DANA team is coordinated by the Maintenance Manager and reports to the General Manager as EOC Coordinator.

Team composition:

- Head, Plumbing division
- Head, Air Conditioning division
- Head, Electrical division
- Head, Building and Facility Maintenance
- Head, Mechanical division
- ECCAA technician

Additional support: Arrange UAV (drone) services to support initial assessment.

## Administrative and logistics arrangements

- Managers and department heads should submit staff contact details and vehicle registration numbers to HR.
- Establish MOU with hardware store or lumberyard for rapid delivery of facility-securing materials under watch/warning conditions.
- Establish MOU with service station for fuel support for GAA and staff vehicles after cyclone passage.
- Arrange nearby accommodation for key staff and their families to support duty continuity during system passage.

## Triggered briefing requirement

Whenever a tropical cyclone warrants a watch advisory for Grenada, the Meteorological Department should brief key staff and issue notifications to all departments.
